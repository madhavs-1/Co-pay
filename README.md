# Google XYZ Resume Formula & Accomplishment Inventory

A reference guide to Google's recommended resume bullet framework and the pre-writing **accomplishment inventory** workflow used to apply it.

---

## Overview

The **Google XYZ formula** is a resume writing framework popularized by **Laszlo Bock**, former Senior Vice President of People Operations at Google. It appears in his 2014 LinkedIn article [*My Personal Formula for a Winning Resume*](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/20140929001534-24454816-my-personal-formula-for-a-better-resume) and his book **Work Rules!**

The canonical phrasing:

> **Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y] by doing [Z]**

In a New York Times interview, Bock also expressed it as:

> **"I accomplished X, relative to Y, by doing Z."**

Google recommends this format because recruiters spend very little time on each resume. Vague duty lists get skimmed past. XYZ forces **outcome + proof + method** into a single scannable line.

---

## The Three Components

### X — What you accomplished (the outcome)

This is **not** a job duty. It is the **positive result** of your work.

| Good X (outcome) | Bad X (duty) |
|---|---|
| Improved portfolio performance | Studied financial performance of companies |
| Reduced logistics costs | Managed supply chain |
| Increased brand recall | Studied branding strategies |
| Selected for leadership program | Member of MLT |

X should answer: **"What got better because I was there?"**

Common outcome categories:

- Revenue or profit growth
- Cost reduction
- Time saved
- Quality or accuracy improved
- Customer or user satisfaction
- Throughput or productivity
- Risk reduced
- Something shipped, launched, or completed

### Y — How success was measured (the metric)

Y provides **quantifiable evidence** that the outcome is real and meaningful.

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Percentages | +12% portfolio performance, −30% ad spend, +25% sales |
| Dollar amounts | $1.2M gain, $900K savings, $500K under budget |
| Counts / scale | 50 op-eds vs. avg of 6, team of 10, 85 customers/day |
| Time | 2 weeks faster, 5 hours/week saved, 18-month program |
| Rates / scores | 100% accuracy, 99.5% fill rate, +25 NPS points |
| Comparisons | vs. peers, vs. industry average, vs. prior period |

**Why baselines matter:** Bock's example — *"Improved portfolio performance by 12% ($1.2M) over one year"* — shows that 12% alone is ambiguous. On a $10M portfolio, $1.2M is significant; on $100, it is not. Include **both relative (%) and absolute ($, count, time) measures** when possible.

Even entry-level roles benefit from specificity:

> Served **85 customers/day with 100% accuracy** (vs. peer average of 70 at 90%).

### Z — How you did it (actions, skills, tools, method)

Z explains **your contribution** — the levers you pulled to produce X and Y.

| Weak Z | Strong Z |
|---|---|
| Used software | Leveraged Python scripts to automate reporting |
| Worked with team | Led cross-functional 10-member team to develop global ad strategy |
| Fixed problems | Designed online auction across multiple vendors |
| Improved process | Implemented agile methodologies and optimized resource allocation |

Z should:

- Name **specific tools** (Salesforce, SAP, SQL, etc.) when relevant
- Highlight **skills** that match the target job description
- Clarify **your role** even on team efforts
- Avoid filler ("worked hard," "was responsible for")

## The Accomplishment Inventory Project

Before writing resume bullets, run an **accomplishment inventory** for **each job**. This is the pre-writing "project" that feeds the XYZ formula.

### Phase 1: Brain dump

For each role, list everything you did — paid work, internships, projects, leadership, volunteer work.

Prompt yourself with:

- What problems did I solve?
- What did I build, launch, or ship?
- What did I improve, reduce, or increase?
- What did I lead or influence?
- What would have gone wrong without me?
- What did managers praise in reviews?
- What numbers appeared in reviews or dashboards?

Sources to mine:

- Performance reviews
- OKRs / KPIs
- Project post-mortems
- Before/after metrics
- Awards, promotions, expanded scope

### Phase 2: Extract X and Y pairs

For each raw item, identify:

1. **X** — What was the outcome?
2. **Y** — How was it measured?
3. **Baseline** — Compared to what? (average, target, prior period)

Example inventory table:

| Raw duty | X (outcome) | Y (metric) | Z (method) — draft |
|---|---|---|---|
| Managed sorority budget | Managed budget effectively | $31K budget; 5% return on idle funds | Invested $10K in high-yield notes |
| Negotiated vendor contracts | Reduced service contract costs | 30% ($500K) reduction | Online auction across vendors |
| Training program | Increased unit proficiency | +20% measured proficiency | Redesigned training for X,000 Marines |

**Rule:** Every bullet should include **at least one number** in Y.

### Phase 3: Write XYZ bullets

Connect inventory rows into full sentences. **Word order is flexible** — all three elements must be present.

**Template variations:**

```
Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y] by doing [Z]
[Action verb] [X] by [Y] by [Z]
[Action verb] to achieve [X], delivering [Y], through [Z]
Improved [Y] by [action Z], contributing to [X]
```

**Marketing examples:**

- Increased page views **(X)** by **23% in six months (Y)** by implementing social media distribution strategies **(Z)**
- Reduced ad spend **(X)** by **30% (Y)** by improving customer targeting **(Z)**

**Sales examples:**

- Increased conversions **(X)** by **28% (Y)** after training five new team members **(Z)**
- Launched new product **(X)** leading to **15% profit increase in Q1 (Y)** by engaging newsletter subscribers **(Z)**

**Customer service examples:**

- Reduced errors **(X)** by **40% (Y)** after creating a new SOP document **(Z)**
- Increased customer satisfaction **(X)** by **18% (Y)** by implementing survey feedback **(Z)**

### Phase 4: Tailor per job

The inventory is your **master list**. For each application:

1. Read the job description
2. Pick 3–5 bullets per role that match required skills and outcomes
3. Swap in **keywords** from the posting
4. Keep each bullet to **~1 line**
5. Lead with your strongest, most relevant metrics

|---|---|
| Managed sorority budget | Managed **$31,000** Spring 2014 budget and invested **$10,000** in idle funds in high-yield capital notes returning **5%** over the year |
| Negotiated service contracts with XYZ | Negotiated **30% ($500k)** reduction in costs with XYZ by designing and using results from an **online auction of multiple vendors** |
| Member of MLT | Selected as **one of 230 participants nationwide** for 18-month professional development program based on leadership potential and academic success |
| Studied branding of XYZ | Led **10-member cross-functional team** to develop global advertising strategy for **$X million** brand, resulting in **25-point brand recall increase**, **12% NPS improvement**, and **18% YoY sales ($XM)** |

## Resume Structure (Google Guidance)

**Section order — experienced candidates:**

1. **Experience** (primary XYZ bullets)
2. Leadership and Awards
3. Education
4. Optional (Projects, Publications, etc.)

**Recent graduates:** Education first, then Experience.

**Formatting rules:**

- Reverse-chronological experience
- Start each bullet with an **action verb**
- Include a **numeral** in every bullet when possible
- Consistent font (Arial, Cambria, Garamond, Times; 11–12pt)
- **One-page PDF** preferred
- Cover letter optional at Google — the resume does the heavy lifting
- No typos, no confidential company data, no first-person pronouns

**Example experience block:**

```
Social Media Marketer, Agency | Oct 2021 – Present
• Increased social media impressions by 35% by creating and posting IG Reels and TikTok
• Reduced planning time by 5 hours/week by implementing a content calendar and scheduling posts
```

## Why It Works

Recruiters silently ask **"So what?"** for every line. XYZ answers three questions in one scan:

1. **What changed?** (X)
2. **How do I know?** (Y)
3. **How did they do it?** (Z)

Compared to other frameworks:

| Framework | Best for |
|---|---|
| **XYZ** | Data-driven roles; quick, metric-heavy bullets |
| **STAR** | Complex stories needing context (Situation, Task, Action, Result) |
| **CAR** | Problem-solving / overcoming obstacles (Challenge, Action, Result) |
| **PAR** | Clean project wins (Project, Action, Result) |

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Listing duties, not outcomes | Ask "What improved?" for every task |
| Metrics with no scale | Add $, count, or comparison baseline |
| Missing Z | Always include how — tools, method, scope |
| Team credit with no individual contribution | Quantify your slice or name your specific role |
| One resume for all jobs | Tailor from inventory per job description |
| Fabricated numbers | Use honest estimates with "approximately" if needed |
| Paragraph-length bullets | One line, scannable |
| Swapping Y and Z | **Y = measurement**, **Z = method** (canonical Bock definition) |

> **Note:** Some blog posts swap Y and Z labels. The official Bock/Google version is: **X** = accomplished, **Y** = measured by, **Z** = by doing.

## Quick Reference

```
FORMULA:  Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y] by doing [Z]

INVENTORY WORKFLOW:
  1. Brain dump all duties and wins per job
  2. For each: identify X (outcome) + Y (number) + Z (how)
  3. Add baselines (vs avg, vs target, vs prior)
  4. Write bullets — order flexible, all 3 parts required
  5. Tailor to each job description

EVERY BULLET NEEDS:
  ✓ Strong action verb
  ✓ At least one number
  ✓ Method / tools / skills
  ✓ One line, scannable
```
